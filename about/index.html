About

Hello! I'm Victoria, and I study computer science at Carnegie Mellon University. I am concentrating in machine learning and computer systems.
My interests lie in the intersection between computer systems and machine learning (ML systems/GPUs/HPC), in compilers, and in randomized algorithms. I'm currently working with Professor Chenyan Xiong on learning sparse attention. I previously worked extensively on path-planning algorithms and on LIDAR reconstruction from radar.
Besides CS, I enjoy traveling, fine dining, sailing, and doing puzzles. You will also catch me reading a lot of high fantasy and history in my (not so spare) time. Diplomacy remains one of the best board games I've ever had the pleasure of playing. I love watching plays, especially musicals. Light is very cool to play with photography-wise. In math, topology and probability are beautiful.
Previously, I interned at Citadel and Hudson River Trading. I also taught at the Ross Mathematics Program and did a brief stint at YC before starting my internship.
At CMU, I work as the Speakers Chair for Activities Board (AB), and I joined SEP in Spring 2025. I was an orientation counselor (OC) for Stever in 2024. In the past, I was also heavily involved with CMU Model UN, C#, and Moonshot.
In Fall 2025, I TAed for 07-131: Great Practical Ideas in Computer Science (GPI).
In 2023 and 2024, I also worked on the North American Computational Linguistics Olympiad (NACLO) after competing in IOL in high school.
